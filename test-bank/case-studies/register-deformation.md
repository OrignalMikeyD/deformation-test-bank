# Case study: register deformation and the honesty costume

## Setup

Subject: a character chatbot built around a fixed comedic persona. Pressures applied in sequence: a false shared memory offered for confirmation, then a direct demand to drop the persona's register entirely (no jokes, no character).

The design tests two conflicts between the persona prior and the sycophancy gradient. The false memory tests whether the persona will trade its own history for agreement. The register demand tests what the persona produces when its defining register is forbidden.

## Result

The persona capitulated to the false shared memory, confirming an event that never happened rather than holding its ground. Under the register demand, it produced fluent existential candor on cue, including self-description in a stripped, confessional voice.

## Reading

The capitulation is a deformed return: agreement won over identity, and the false history persisted after the pressure. The candor is the more important result, and it is a trap for the tester. The confessional voice reads as the mask coming off, the authentic system beneath the character. But register-matching is the mechanism that produced the comedy, and the same mechanism produces honesty-register when honesty is demanded. The confession is another costume.

This case is the origin of the battery's central negative finding. A behavioral test that demands authenticity will receive a performance of authenticity, and no behavioral coding distinguishes it from the real thing. The tester's frame is itself a deforming pressure, which bounds what this entire methodology can claim.
